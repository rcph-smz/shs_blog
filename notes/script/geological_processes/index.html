geological processes
Earthquake
Earthquake is a sudden movement of ground along the faults lines when two blocks of the earth suddenly slip past one another (Plate boundaries are always faults, but not all faults are plate boundaries)
Most reasons earthquake happens is when: - Volcanic Eruption Occur, which creates Volcanic Earthquake - Plate tectonic boundaries slips together, that could lead into Tectonic Earthquake - and Induced Earthquakes which is cause by human activity like tunnel construction and filling reservoirs
Earthquake could also cause some hazards like: - Ground rupture/surface fault - Ground Shaking - liquefaction - Tsunami, etc Just to name a few
Ground Shaking
Ground Shaking is a up, down and sideways vibration of the ground to break apart during an earthquake.
The hazards of ground shaking are mostly damaging or collapsing tall buildings ,it can also cause some hazards like landslides, mudslides, avalanches, melting or liquefaction.
Surface faulting
Surface fautling is a Deformation of a ground that marks as a fault on the earths surface
Surface faulting hazard may lead you to future destruction to any nearby establishment
Landslides
A landslide is the movement of rock and/or soil, down a slope
some hazards of landslides are : - rockfalls in which a rock falling downslopes, - slope failure in which the slope of very steep area collapse, - debris flows in which a fast-moving landslide made up of liquefied, soft, saturated mass. - and avalanche in which a mass of snow, ice, and rocks falling rapidly down a mountainside.
liquefaction
liquefaction is a phenomenon that is cause by earthquake shaking,it behaves like a liquid in which the ground-surface is saturated
liquefaction hazard can act as a quick sand and has a ability to sink building, cars, houses, and any heavy objects.
Tsunami
Series of waves caused commonly by an earthquake under the sea.
Tsunami hazards can cause flooding, wave impacts, erosion, strong currents, and floating debris
